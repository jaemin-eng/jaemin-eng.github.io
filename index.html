Sep 2019 - Present

University of Toronto

Ph.D. Candidate

Under the guidance of my advisor, Prof. George V. Eleftheriades, my Ph.D. research focused on advancing Huygens' metasurfaces to solve a fundamental challenge in modern antenna systems: enhancing the scan range of phased-array antennas. This work is directly applicable to creating more compact, cost-effective, and high-performance beam-steering systems for next-generation applications like LEO satellite constellations, 5G/6G communications, and advanced radar platforms.

 

Key Contributions:
  • Designed and experimentally verified a compact, near-field two-lens HMS system to double the scan-angle of phased arrays. This approach was successfully scaled to E-band (73 GHz), demonstrating its feasibility for next-generation communication systems. (View Publication)
  • Developed a novel dual-polarized Huygens' metasurface that achieves wide-angle scanning without the directivity loss typically found in other lens-based solutions for scan-angle enhancement. This design maintains high performance for both orthogonal polarizations, a critical feature for advanced communication and sensing platforms. (View Publication)
